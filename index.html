Why we teach differently

Most CS programs teach students to follow steps. We teach them to think. There's a meaningful difference between a student who can copy a tutorial and one who can build something from scratch.

We deliberately remove complexity — not because rigor is bad, but because unnecessary complexity is the enemy of real understanding.

"The scholar gains day-by-day. The maestro loses day-by-day. Unlearning and undoing are as necessary as learning."

01
First principles, always

We teach the underlying concept before the abstraction. Students understand why, not just how.

02
Build real things

Every student ships something real — websites, games, tools, apps. Not exercises. Actual projects with actual users.

03
Clarity over complexity

We don't teach frameworks before fundamentals. Simple, readable, intentional code is the goal.

04
AI-native education

We teach students to use AI tools responsibly while keeping deep conceptual understanding intact.
